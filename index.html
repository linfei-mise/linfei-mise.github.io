Fei Lin (林飞)

Ph.D. Student Macau University of Science and Technology

Welcome. I am Fei Lin, a 3rd-year Ph.D. student in Intelligence Science and Systems at the Department of Engineering Science, Faculty of Innovation Engineering, Macau University of Science and Technology (MUST). I am also affiliated with the Macau Institute of System Engineering (MISE). I am a member of AAAS, IEEE, AIChE, and ACM. I am fortunate to be supervised by Distinguished Prof. Fei-Yue Wang (AAAS Fellow, ASME Fellow, IFAC Fellow, INCOSE Fellow, IEEE Fellow).

My research centers on AI for Science (AI4S), with primary focus on: AI for Drug Discovery; AI for Covalent Organic Frameworks (COF) / Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOF); and Self-driving Laboratories (SDL) — integrating embodied intelligence into chemistry and materials science through Vision-Language-Action models for autonomous wet-lab experimentation. I have also been continuously engaged in research on Parallel Intelligence.

Previously, I worked on Embodied Agents and Agentic Workflows, primarily applied to UAV autonomous perception and action. During my Master's at MUST, I focused on medical image segmentation for interventional puncture surgery robots. I co-founded DeepYoke, a non-profit AI4S research initiative, with collaborators from SJTU, Shanghai AI Lab, CASIA, ZGC Academy, and IPE-CAS.
